Physics & Data Engine Overview
Simulation Architecture

How Weather Data Becomes
Expected Energy Output

See the technical workflow of our AI-driven engine. We process raw GHI parameters into bankable P50/P90 energy yields and financial projections.

01

GHI Data Aggregation

25+ Years of Historical Climate Processing

  • Acquire site-specific coordinates via map pin or direct input.
  • Fetch 25+ years of Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) data to map true weather trends.
  • Extract granular hour-by-hour metrics including ambient temperature and diffuse radiation.
02

POA Irradiance Calculation

Determining True Solar Exposure

  • Translate GHI into Plane of Array (POA) irradiance using structural Tilt and Azimuth parameters.
  • Apply transposition algorithms to account for the physical angle of the solar arrays.
03

Dynamic Loss Modeling

Applying Engineering Derates

  • Calculate thermal losses dynamically hour-by-hour using ambient weather vs panel NOCT ratings.
  • Factor in geographical soiling, wiring (ohmic) resistance, and inverter clipping/efficiency limits.
System_Parameters.cfg
04

Expected Energy Numbers

P50 vs. P90 Confidence Intervals

  • P50 Yield: The baseline 50% probability estimate, indicating standard expected generation (kWh/year).
  • P90 Yield: The conservative 90% confidence estimate used by banks to underwrite solar financing.
05

Financial Analysis

ROI & Investment Economics

  • Translate generation data into monetary value using local grid tariffs and system costs.
  • Automatically calculate critical sales metrics: Payback Period, IRR, and 25-Year Lifecycle Savings.
