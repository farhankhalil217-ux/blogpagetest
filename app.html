About Solar Metrix Pro

Platform Architecture & Data Sources

Solar Metrix Pro is an advanced yield simulation engine. This tool is trained on 25+ years of historical data (2000-Present) and implements AI-based Weather physics to accurately predict Plane of Array (POA) irradiance and Energy generation.

AI Physics Engine

Dynamically models heat & soiling losses hour-by-hour using trained historical patterns.

25+ Year Dataset

Audit covers 2000 to the previous year, ensuring robust P50/P90 probability calculations.

Important Note on Financials:
The financial module uses a simplified yield-based model: ROI = (Total Energy Yield × Grid Rate) / System Cost. It assumes all generated energy is valuable (either self-consumed or exported).

Technical Parameters Guide

Input Configuration Help

Temperature Coefficient (Pmax)

This value determines how much efficiency your solar panel loses as it gets hot. It is always a negative number.

Example: If your datasheet says -0.35 %/°C, it means for every degree above 25°C, the panel loses 0.35% power.

NOCT (Nominal Operating Cell Temp)

Measures how hot a specific solar panel gets under normal operating conditions.

Typical Value: Most modern panels are between 42°C and 45°C.
